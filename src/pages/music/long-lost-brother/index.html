I released this song back in 2012 as my first single upon arriving back home to the Bahamas after college. I was inspired by my visit to Jamaica and experiencing the vibrancy of the culture and people.

The Jamaica that I experienced seem to be a time capsule, there were reminders of the past everywhere, which reminded me of how closely connected we are historically and spiritually. I walked on the same paths of the prophet Robert nester Marley and I saw what he saw, I felt his spirit.

The actual music is reminiscent of the soul of Jamaica with the influence of rock steady rhythms and grooves. Mean while the lyrics are a message, not only for Bahamians but everyone of the African diaspora, who has a common history of slavery and are still dealing with the psychological impact it has had on us all, black and white alike.

My favorite line in the song is “without the cooperation of our so demise, they would have never received the prize.” This shows that humanity is always responsible for the suffering of Humanity.

The only reason we suffer is because we allow it to happen. We always have the power to change because we always have a choice. I was compelled to bridge a gap between barriers of geography and culture, doing away with the idea of separation but rather creating a sense of unity and wholeness.

If we change our mindset, we can experience the world as one creation that is not segregated within itself, focusing on the similarities of the human family and celebrate the uniqueness of our differences.
